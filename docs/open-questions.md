# Open Questions — What Is Still Unresolved and Worth Serious Work

> This page explains the major open questions that still remain inside the Sal-Meter Kernel Program.  
> It is written for researchers, scholars, engineers, PI-level readers, and serious technical observers who want to understand where the frontier is still genuinely unresolved.  
> It is not a claim of completion. It is a map of burden.

---

## 1. Why This Page Exists

A project becomes easier to misunderstand when people can see its language but cannot see its unresolved questions.

Some readers then assume too much has already been proven.  
Others assume too little is real to deserve attention.  
Both mistakes weaken the field.

This page exists to prevent that.

Its purpose is simple:

**to show that the Sal-Meter Kernel Program is not asking people to admire a finished result, but to face a set of hard questions that still require serious work.**

That matters because the right researcher, scholar, or engineer is often drawn not to a completed answer, but to a burden that is still alive.

---

## 2. How to Read This Page

These are **open questions**, not soft slogans.

That means:

- they are not decorative
- they are not proof statements
- they are not placeholders for hype
- they are not excuses for vagueness

They are unresolved burdens.

A serious reader should approach them with three attitudes at once:

- openness
- skepticism
- discipline

This page exists so that the frontier remains visible as a frontier.

---

## 3. The Deepest Open Question

The deepest open question is still this:

**Can a bounded CAIS-aligned molecular-electrochemical interface become reproducible enough to support honest state-separability under conditions strict enough to justify broader opening?**

Everything else branches from that.

If the answer is no, that matters.  
If the answer is partially yes, that matters.  
If the answer becomes strongly yes under discipline, that matters even more.

But at the current stage, the question remains open.

---

## 4. Major Open Questions

## 4.1 Does the kernel genuinely exist as a stable interface?

This is more fundamental than most readers first realize.

The question is not merely whether interesting behavior can be observed.

The question is:

- does the interface remain real under repeat conditions?
- does it survive handling variation?
- does it survive baseline pressure?
- does it remain more than an artifact of a narrow setup?
- does it deserve the layers built above it?

This remains open.

---

## 4.2 What are the dominant drift sources?

Drift is one of the strongest enemies of false confidence.

Open questions include:

- where is drift entering the system?
- which parts of the setup are most sensitive?
- what is chemical drift and what is instrument drift?
- what looks like state change but may actually be time-dependent instability?
- what level of drift is acceptable for the current stage?

Until drift is better bounded, many stronger claims remain premature.

---

## 4.3 How stable is the baseline, really?

A weak baseline can make the rest of the stack look more meaningful than it is.

Open questions include:

- what counts as a stable baseline in this context?
- how much baseline variation is tolerable?
- when does baseline instability invalidate downstream interpretation?
- are repeated baseline conditions meaningfully comparable?
- which baseline behaviors are structural, and which are accidental?

This remains one of the core unresolved burdens.

---

## 4.4 Can state-separability be shown honestly rather than cosmetically?

This is a central frontier question.

Open questions include:

- what counts as the minimum honest separability claim?
- how much apparent separation is actually preprocessing-dependent?
- which differences survive stricter validation?
- where does false clarity enter?
- what would count as a clean negative result rather than a confusing partial one?

State-separability is not just about getting distinction.
It is about earning distinction.

---

## 4.5 How dangerous is preprocessing?

Preprocessing may clarify, but it may also hallucinate structure.

Open questions include:

- which preprocessing steps are currently safest?
- which steps are likely to create false separability?
- how much normalization is too much?
- when does the pipeline become more confident than the raw structure deserves?
- how can preprocessing remain honest under weak-signal conditions?

This is one of the most important unresolved technical burdens.

---

## 4.6 Are the channels really independent?

A multi-channel architecture only deserves trust if coexistence does not hide contamination.

Open questions include:

- where can leak enter?
- where can cross-talk mimic meaningful structure?
- how should independence actually be tested?
- what counts as acceptable separation between channels?
- what failures are most likely to remain invisible at first?

This burden is still open.

---

## 4.7 What negative controls are mandatory?

A mature frontier is often defined not only by what it can show, but by what it insists on ruling out.

Open questions include:

- which negative controls are non-negotiable?
- what would falsify the stronger interpretations?
- what controls protect against overread signal behavior?
- what kinds of null outcomes should be actively designed for?
- what must fail cleanly before stronger claims become credible?

This is one of the places where rigor becomes love in technical form.

---

## 4.8 What counts as enough for Lock 1?

Lock 1 asks whether the interface exists.

But the threshold still requires sharper working understanding.

Open questions include:

- what evidence is enough to treat the interface as real?
- what evidence is still too weak?
- what kind of reproducibility is minimally required?
- what ambiguities must be reduced first?
- what would make a Lock 1 claim premature?

This question matters because existence is the first threshold the project must earn.

---

## 4.9 What counts as enough for Lock 2?

Even if the interface exists, it may still be too weak, too unstable, or too narrow to justify broader opening.

Open questions include:

- what makes the interface matter?
- what level of stability and interpretive discipline is enough to widen the field?
- what would make broader opening irresponsible?
- what evidence shows that widening the field would amplify signal rather than noise?
- what should remain internal longer?

This is still open.

---

## 4.10 What should broader opening actually mean?

Many readers assume broader opening is a simple public release condition.

It is not.

Open questions include:

- what permissions, if any, should broader opening create?
- what should still remain bounded even after opening?
- how can broader opening occur without creating naming confusion, authority drift, or false compliance assumptions?
- what kind of external participation should come first?
- how can opening remain non-exclusive without becoming structurally loose?

This is both a governance and architecture question.

---

## 4.11 How should public language remain proportional to stage?

This project can be damaged not only by technical failure, but by language outrunning proof.

Open questions include:

- what is the strongest safe language right now?
- what terms should be avoided until later?
- how should researchers and engineers describe the project without distortion?
- how do we protect the project from both inflation and unnecessary flattening?
- how do we keep moral seriousness visible without weakening scientific discipline?

This question is still alive and will remain important.

---

## 4.12 What would cross-lab reproducibility actually require?

A result that survives only in one local context is still too weak for stronger ambition.

Open questions include:

- which protocol elements must be fixed before cross-lab work becomes meaningful?
- what must become deterministic?
- what must be machine-readable or more tightly standardized?
- what makes a result transportable rather than local?
- what is the minimum reproducibility structure required for serious external validation?

This is one of the biggest future burdens.

---

## 4.13 What is the correct boundary between signal and interpretation?

This is not merely technical.
It is one of the project’s deepest intellectual burdens.

Open questions include:

- what does raw signal behavior justify?
- what does it not yet justify?
- when does interpretive language become too strong?
- how do we distinguish interface-level findings from final semantic claims?
- how do we prevent the project from collapsing either into empty metaphysics or blind instrumentism?

This is exactly where researchers, scholars, and validation-minded builders may matter most.

---

## 5. Why These Questions Matter

These questions matter because the future is increasingly shaped by systems that affect human states while remaining only weakly accountable to the condition of the beings they reorganize.

That means the absence of disciplined state-sensitive questions is no longer a minor academic gap.

It is becoming a structural problem.

The Sal-Meter Kernel Program matters not because it claims to have solved that problem already, but because it tries to bring the burden into visible form.

That is why the open questions matter so much.

They are where the future is still undecided.
