## 1. Why These Two Roles Exist

Sal-Meter is not being built as a public-facing competition at the current stage.

The current priority is to stabilize the kernel internally first:

- measurement conditions
- device-side repeatability
- SOP consistency
- data structure
- metadata discipline
- QC
- leakage prevention
- evaluation integrity
- reproducibility packaging

That is why the present operating structure is explicitly organized around **two internal leads**:

- **ESL** for physical and measurement consistency
- **EStL** for evidence consistency and standardization discipline

These roles are not symbolic titles.

They exist because a frontier measurement program usually fails in one of two ways:

1. the signal itself is unstable, operator-dependent, or physically unreliable  
2. the evidence around the signal is cosmetically improved while structurally broken underneath

This two-lead structure exists to prevent both failures at the same time.

## 2. Current Recruitment Posture

This recruitment should be read as:

- **internal-first**
- **kernel-first**
- **pre-opening**
- **research-stage**
- **non-clinical**

This is not a call for large teams, broad public participation, or generalized hype.

## 3. The Two-Lead Structure

## 3.1 Electrochemical Systems Lead (ESL)

The ESL owns the **physical consistency** of the kernel.

This includes responsibility for:

- electrochemical measurement workflow design
- potentiostat / electrode / bench stability
- cartridge or chamber-side logic
- drift control
- fouling response
- repeatability troubleshooting
- operating procedure stability
- signal survival under real measurement conditions
- internal execution support for early kernel-stage runs

The ESL protects the project from becoming interpretive theater built on unstable measurement conditions.

### Best-fit background for ESL

You may fit ESL if your strongest background is in areas such as:

- electrochemistry
- analytical chemistry
- biosensors
- redox measurement
- electrode behavior
- prototype instrumentation
- device-side experimental systems
- bench setup stabilization
- cartridge / sensing workflow design

---

## 3.2 Evidence & Standardization Lead (EStL)

The EStL owns the **evidence consistency** of the kernel.

This includes responsibility for:

- metadata schema
- QC framework
- logging discipline
- holdout structure
- leakage prevention
- evidence packaging
- evaluation integrity
- release logic
- standard documentation
- version-aware documentation discipline
- reproducibility package organization

The EStL protects the project from producing attractive-looking outputs with structurally weak evidence underneath.

### Best-fit background for EStL

You may fit EStL if your strongest background is in areas such as:

- experimental data systems
- applied statistics
- validation pipelines
- audit trails
- metadata design
- schema design
- QC systems
- reproducibility engineering
- benchmark discipline
- evidence review structure

---

## 4. Why the Split Matters

These two roles should **not** be collapsed into one vague “program manager” or one generic “research lead.”

The split matters because the kernel must be stabilized on two fronts at once:

- the **signal side**
- the **evidence side**

A project can have clean chemistry but weak metadata.
It can have elegant instrumentation but poor QC.
It can have strong preprocessing but hidden leakage.
It can have attractive plots but unrepeatable acquisition.

The two-lead structure exists because the physical system and the evidence system must both survive.

---

## 5. First 30-Day Mission

The first 30 days are not about publishing a dramatic paper or announcing a breakthrough.

The first 30-day target is:

# Reproducibility Pack v1.0

This is the right early mission because everything later depends on it.

### Minimum components of the first 30-day pack

- SOP v1.0 draft
- metadata schema
- session ID discipline
- failure log format
- drift note structure
- role-based review flow
- first internal QC rhythm
- first stable naming and version logic

Without these, later pilot work, cross-session comparison, and external reproducibility all remain weak.

---

## 6. First 90-Day Mission

The first 90 days should move from structural setup toward the first credible internal reproducibility package.

### Direction of work in this stage

- repeated sessions under interpretable conditions
- zero essential metadata loss
- traceable QC structure
- usable drift reports
- evidence packaging that can survive serious review
- a review rhythm that can continue through the kernel stages without confusion

The point is not to create inflated certainty.
The point is to make sure later claims rest on something that can survive internal scrutiny.

---

## 7. What Success Looks Like

A strong ESL / EStL pair should make the following things visibly stronger:

### ESL-side signals of success

- measurement setup becomes less operator-dependent
- drift is no longer treated as vague annoyance but as a mapped condition
- bench conditions become reproducible enough to compare seriously
- device-side SOPs begin to feel lockable rather than improvised
- acquisition becomes more honest, not more theatrical

### EStL-side signals of success

- missing or weak metadata stops being tolerated
- QC becomes explicit rather than assumed
- holdout and leakage logic become discussable, testable, and visible
- versioning stops drifting
- evidence release discipline becomes difficult to bypass

## 9. Who Should Seriously Consider Contacting Us

You should seriously consider contacting us if the following description feels accurate:

### You may fit ESL if:

- you have hands-on experience in electrochemical or biosensor measurement systems
- you think naturally in terms of drift, fouling, electrode state, repeatability, and SOP lock
- you are willing to be accountable for physical measurement consistency
- you care more about a stable signal than a dramatic narrative

### You may fit EStL if:

- you think in terms of evidence integrity before performance
- you know how schema design, QC, holdout logic, and leakage prevention shape conclusions
- you are comfortable stopping a project from lying to itself
- you care more about clean evaluation than attractive claims

### You may fit either role if:

- you can own one burden clearly
- you are comfortable with an internal-first stage
- you understand that real credibility is earned before public scale
- you can strengthen a frontier program without inflating it

## 10. A Realistic Minimum Burden

A serious first engagement does not require an entire new center or a large institutional announcement.

A realistic beginning may be as small as:

- one lead
- one researcher / engineer
- one bounded module
- one 30-day stabilization objective
- one 90-day evidence milestone

This is a kernel-building program.
The relevant question is not scale at the beginning.
The relevant question is whether one person can lock one burden well.

## 12. How to Write the 1-Page Execution Note

The strongest note is usually short, concrete, and burden-aware.

A useful note should answer questions such as:

- What would you stabilize first?
- What would you refuse to assume?
- What would you measure before making any performance statement?
- What kind of failure would you try to surface early?
- What would count as a meaningful first 30-day improvement?

Weak notes tend to sound like inspiration.
Strong notes sound like controlled reduction of uncertainty.

## 14. Public Position vs Real Position

In conventional programs, outside visibility often comes before internal clarity.

This program is trying to resist that pattern.

The goal is not to hire publicly and improvise internally.
The goal is to stabilize internally first and widen later only if the kernel deserves it.

That is why the present recruitment language must stay disciplined.

Good language now is:

- internal reproducibility kernel
- pre-opening
- research-stage
- non-clinical
- non-diagnostic
- non-therapeutic
- bounded pilot
- evidence discipline
- measurement consistency

Bad language now is:

- ready-for-launch
- broad competition now open
- certified system
- diagnostic device
- fully proven platform
- public participation already active

## 16. If You Are Unsure Whether You Fit

If you are unsure whether you fit, ask a smaller question first:

**Can I reduce one uncertainty in this kernel honestly within 30 days?**

If the answer is yes, you may fit.
If the answer depends on public scale, large budgets, prestige language, or symbolic positioning first, then the fit is probably weak at this stage.

The present need is not broad recruitment.
